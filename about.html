Burlington, Vermont

A small house
for small farms.

GreenKart began with a station wagon, six regular customers, and a conviction that most organic eCommerce was putting the wrong word first. The word that mattered to us — still — is small. Small farms. Small batches. Small distances from the field to your table.

Two years on there are fourteen thousand of you, one hundred and twenty cooperative farms within a day's drive of Burlington, and a team of twenty-two who have, at one point or another, all driven the station wagon.

Our standards are straightforward. Every grower in our network holds an independent organic certification. Nothing we sell travels further than it has to, and nothing sits in cold storage waiting for a listing to refresh. We take ten percent of the sale price; the farmer takes the rest.

We still answer the phone ourselves. It rings less than you might expect, and we like it that way — it usually means the boxes are arriving on time.

How we got here

A short history, honestly told.

2023

A station wagon and six customers.

Our first delivery route ran three farms, six houses, and a Sunday morning. The cooler went in the passenger seat.

2024

Twelve farms, one thousand subscribers.

Moved into a converted dairy barn on Maple Grove Road and published our first seasonal journal.

2025

B-Corp certification.

Independent audit confirmed fair wage, closed-loop packaging, and a carbon-neutral delivery network.

2026

120 farms, 24 states.

Volume Twelve of the seasonal journal goes out on the first Saturday of April — the day you are, probably, reading this.
